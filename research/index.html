Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of physics and computer science, with a particular focus on quantum information, condensed matter physics, and machine learning methods applied to physical systems.

During my undergraduate studies, I completed my final project under the supervision of Professor Michael Hilke. I explored quantum computing through the lens of topological protection. Specifically, I studied how topological structures such as the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger (SSH) model can protect qubits from decoherence. I developed a protocol to fine-tune the SSH model such that, even with significant coupling to the environment, the qubit remains robustly protected.

After graduation, I joined the Kastler Brossel Laboratory in Paris as a research intern in the Quantum Fluid of Light group led by Professor Quentin Glorieux. There, I developed a machine learning tool to better characterize quantum fluids in hot atomic vapors.

I am currently a Ph.D. student in the group of Professor Hong Guo at McGill University, known for its leading contributions to computational condensed matter physics. My doctoral work is still in development, and I am exploring problems at the interface of:

  • Quantum field theory
  • Condensed matter physics
  • High-energy physics
  • Machine learning
  • Computational physics
