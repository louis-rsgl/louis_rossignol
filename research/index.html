Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of physics and computer science

For my undergraduate final project, I was supervised by Professor Michael Hilke. One possible field that complies with my research interests is Quantum Computing. In this project, I studied how topological structures like the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger model can provide protection to quantum bits and therefore be a possible solution for decoherence proof quantum computing. I developped a protocole to fine-tune the SSH model such that even with a significant coupling with the environment the qubit remains protected.

After graduation, I interned at the Kasler Brossel Laboratory in the Quantum Fluid of Light group under the supervision of Professor Quentin Glorieux in Paris, France. I developed a machine learning tool to better characterize quantum fluids in hot atomic vapor.

For my PhD, I chose the research group of Professor Hong Guo as it is renowned for its significant contributions to computational condensed matter physics. I am still working on finding my specific Ph.D subject. I am exploring problems relating to:

  • Quantum field theory
  • Condensed matter physics
  • High-energy physics
  • Machine learning
  • Computational Physics
